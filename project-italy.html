Project Overview

During my work at Remote Sensing Solutions, I developed an operational drought forecasting system as part of the EOAgri Twin project for ESA's Destination Earth initiative. The system provides 1-6 month ahead forecasts of key agricultural indicators—Land Surface Temperature (LST), Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), and Normalized Difference Infrared Index (NDII)—which serve as inputs to a comprehensive drought monitoring model for agricultural lands across Europe and Africa.

Core Innovation: Scenario-Based Forecasting

The key innovation of this system is its ability to generate "what-if" scenario forecasts that answer critical questions for agricultural decision-makers:

  • What will vegetation health look like in 3 months if we experience drought conditions?
  • How will crop stress evolve under extreme wet conditions?
  • What is the range of possible outcomes given precipitation uncertainty?

This scenario-based approach provides five distinct forecasts per indicator:

  • Extreme Dry: Representing severe drought conditions (5th percentile precipitation)
  • Dry: Below-average precipitation (20th percentile)
  • Baseline: Climatological average precipitation
  • Wet: Above-average precipitation (80th percentile)
  • Extreme Wet: Flood-risk conditions (95th percentile)

Methodology & Model Architecture

The forecasting system employs an ensemble modeling framework combining XGBoost and ElasticNet regression, with optimal weights determined individually for each:

  • Geographic region (Italy, Germany, Kenya)
  • Target variable (LST, NDVI, NDII)
  • Forecast horizon (1-6 months ahead)

This results in 18 specialized ensemble models, each tuned to the specific spatiotemporal characteristics of its prediction task.

Input Features

The models utilize two main feature categories:

  • Historical observations (lagged 1-12 months): Target variable values, Precipitation totals, Rolling means and standard deviations.
  • Future precipitation scenarios: Location-specific monthly climatology (2001-2022) scaled by regional scenario multipliers.

Spatially-Adaptive Scenario Generation

A critical challenge addressed by this methodology is the heterogeneity of climate conditions within each country. Simply applying uniform scenario definitions nationwide would produce unrealistic forecasts.

Solution: Regional Scenario Calibration

  • Stratified spatial sampling: Each country is divided into 1° × 1° grid cells, with 5 representative training locations selected per cell.
  • Location-specific distributions: Monthly precipitation climatology is modeled using gamma distributions to account for positive-skewed data.
  • Regional scenario multipliers: Scenario thresholds are calculated for each location, then averaged within each 1° × 1° region.

Precipitation_scenario = Climatology_mean + (Climatology_std × Regional_multiplier)

This approach ensures that an "extreme dry" scenario represents genuinely extreme conditions for both arid zones (e.g., 5mm) and wet zones (e.g., 80mm).

Spatial Resolution and Coverage

Forecasts are generated at 0.01° spatial resolution (~1km) exclusively over agricultural lands, identified using Copernicus Land Cover classification. This high-resolution approach provides actionable information for farm-scale decision-making.

Seasonal Considerations

Training data is filtered to the growing season (May-October in Europe), with forecast horizon-dependent adjustments to optimize prediction accuracy during critical crop development periods.

Model Performance

Example for LST (3-month ahead, Italy):

  • Test R²: 0.83
  • Test MAE: 2.5°C
  • Scenario consistency: 85% (predictions maintain expected ordering across scenarios)

Key findings:

  • Ensemble models consistently outperform individual components.
  • Future precipitation scenarios rank as the 2nd most important feature (18% importance).
  • Forecast skill decreases gracefully with increasing horizon (R² = 0.88 at 1-month → 0.78 at 6-months).

Operational Deployment

The system generates monthly operational forecasts as multi-band GeoTIFF rasters for seamless integration into the EOAgri Twin platform, providing five scenario forecasts per indicator with spatial uncertainty quantification (±1σ bounds).
